<h1> How do CAFOs affect livestock Animals? </h1>
  <h2>Living Conditions</h2>
<p> Livestock animals often live among manure, feed, urine, and carcasses of other animals. In some cases, animals may even be caged, chained, or tethered, unable to move their bodies.  Animals do not graze or roam outdoors. They are kept in strictly controlled, warehouse-style facilities where natural behaviors are highly limited.</p>

<h2> Health Effects </h2>
  <p>   Confining a large number of animals in a small land area can be seriously hazardous for the well-being of animals. Poor ventilation causes buildup of toxic gases such as ammonia, ​hydrogen sulfate, and methane, all of which can reach levels that irritate the animals’ eyes and lungs or even become toxic.  Ammonia also damages respiratory tissues, making animals more vulnerable to infections. Various strains of bacteria begin to breed in the crowded environment. This toxic setting leads animals to become aggressive. They start to attack one another, which causes broken bones, bruises, cuts, and in some cases, death.</p>
