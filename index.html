How Do CAFOs Affect Livestock Animals?

Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations, or CAFOs, can affect livestock through crowded housing, limited movement, and exposure to poor air quality and waste buildup.

Living Conditions

Livestock animals often live among manure, feed, urine, and carcasses of other animals. In some cases, animals may even be caged, chained, or tethered, unable to move their bodies. Animals do not graze or roam outdoors. They are kept in strictly controlled, warehouse-style facilities where natural behaviors are highly limited.

These conditions can make it harder for animals to rest, move, and behave naturally.
